How to Create Strong Passwords

Learn best practices for password security

Why Strong Passwords Matter

In today's digital age, your passwords are the first line of defense against unauthorized access to your personal information, financial accounts, and online identity. Weak passwords can be cracked in seconds, while strong passwords provide robust protection against cyber threats.

Characteristics of a Strong Password

A strong password should have the following characteristics:

  • Length: At least 12-16 characters long. Longer passwords are exponentially harder to crack.
  • Complexity: Mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols.
  • Unpredictability: Avoid common words, phrases, or patterns that can be guessed.
  • Uniqueness: Use a different password for each account to prevent credential stuffing attacks.
  • Randomness: Random character combinations are much more secure than memorable phrases.

What to Avoid

When creating passwords, steer clear of these common mistakes:

  • Personal information (names, birthdays, addresses)
  • Dictionary words or common phrases
  • Sequential characters (123456, abcdef)
  • Repeated characters (aaaaaa, 111111)
  • Keyboard patterns (qwerty, asdfgh)
  • Common substitutions (P@ssw0rd)
