History of Kenya

Journey Through Time

Ancient Origins

Kenya is believed to be the birthplace of humanity. The Great Rift Valley has yielded fossils dating back millions of years, including some of the earliest hominid remains ever discovered.

2M BCE

Swahili Coast Trading

Arab and Persian traders established settlements along the Kenyan coast, creating a vibrant Swahili culture. Cities like Mombasa and Lamu became major trading centers for gold, ivory, and spices.

700 CE

Portuguese Arrival

Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama arrived in 1498, beginning a period of European influence. Fort Jesus in Mombasa, built in 1593, stands as a testament to this era.

1498

British Colonization

Kenya became a British protectorate in 1895 and a colony in 1920. The Kenya-Uganda Railway was completed in 1901, opening up the interior for European settlement and trade.

1895

Mau Mau Uprising

The Mau Mau rebellion against British colonial rule began, led primarily by the Kikuyu people. This armed resistance movement was crucial in Kenya's path to independence.

1952

Independence & Republic

Kenya gained independence from Britain on December 12, 1963, with Jomo Kenyatta as the first Prime Minister. A year later, Kenya became a republic with Kenyatta as its first President.

1963

Modern Kenya

Kenya has emerged as East Africa's economic powerhouse, a leader in technology innovation (M-Pesa), and a major tourist destination. Nairobi is now a vibrant hub for business and culture in Africa.

2000s
